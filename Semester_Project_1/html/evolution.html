Evolution

This is a fossile of one of the hunters of the sea, its approximately 140 million years old, and this creature of the sea was a ferocious hunter. This is one of very few complete fossiles in the world, and the only one in all of Europe learn more about how this creature lived, and why it (thankfully) isn't around anymore here with us in the evolution department!

This T-Rex stands at 4 meters tall, and is 12 meters long. During its time it was one of the most feared predators in the world and few dared stand against it. This fossile is among the very few complete ones in the world, and seeing it in person is definetly an experience to write home about! Delve into the heart of the Department of evolution and see if you have what it takes to stand against this ferocious monster!

This family of brontosaurus stand at over 10 meters tall with its neck streched out. This giant of a herbivore and it's family is the pride and joy of this department. It was discovered by some of our very own researchers on an expedition to Serbia. One of the researchers from that expedition now acts as the tour guide for this department, so come join her and find out what a real life Indiana Jones is like!

These skeletons show our incredible journey from tiny apes to humans standing at the very top of the food chain! Learn how we went from one of the worlds weakest creatures, to the strongest in record time!
