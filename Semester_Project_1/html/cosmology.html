Cosmology

This is one of the astronaut costumes currently being exhibited at the musem, it previously belonged to Neil armstrong, the first man on the moon and has been left in our care for the past 3 years. You can learn more about the first ever moon landing and the man who wore this here with us at the musem.

This is part of our exploratorium section, this facility showcases the stars and planets of the universe, videos, audio tours and scaled models can be found here, the unique atmosphere is also made to help simulate parts of what its like to be in outer space. It's an exhibit thats definetly worth taking part in!

Inside the planetarium this incredible contraption displays the vast reaches of space, through lasers and light it can take you anywhere in the galaxy, move through the milkyway, travel to the moon or be the first person to reach mars. It's all part of the exhibit and our visitors are free to suggest stars, planets or even systems they would like to visit during our open Q&A at the end!

This is the planetarium itself, this massive ball of magic has been the pride and joy of the Cosmology department for years now, and will continue to be so for years to come. If you've ever wondered what it's like to be an astronaut this is definetly the place to visit!
